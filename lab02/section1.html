Background

Forest canopies are rarely uniform. Gaps are openings created by windthrow, disease, fire, or harvest, reshaping understory environments and influence regeneration dynamics in Pacific Northwest forests. They alter light, temperature, and moisture, forming microclimatic gradients between the gap center and its shaded edges.

Summary of Forest Gap Factors and Ecological Effects

Factor Change in Gaps Ecological Effect
Light Higher in gap centers; lower near edges Boosts seedling photosynthesis and growth
Temperature Wider daily range (warmer day, cooler night) Affects respiration and moisture balance
Soil Moisture Can decline with evaporation in large gaps Limits seedling survival during dry periods
