Key Findings & Applications

Field observations and analysis revealed non-linear gap effects on regeneration. Extremely large openings tend to overheat and dry soils, while moderate-sized gaps provide an ideal balance of light and moisture. These results emphasize that canopy gaps are not just random disturbances but essential drivers of forest diversity and structure.

Silviculture & Management Implications

  • Apply variable-density thinning (VDT) to create diverse canopy structures.
  • Design gap mosaics that mimic natural disturbance patterns.
  • Monitor regeneration outcomes and adapt thinning intervals to sustain heterogeneity.
